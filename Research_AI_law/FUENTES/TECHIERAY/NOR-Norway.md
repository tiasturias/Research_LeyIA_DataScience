[29 September 2025] Norwegian Data Protection Authority submits consultation statement on proposed implementing act of EU AI Act:
The Norwegian Data Protection Authority (Datatilsynet) has submitted a consultation statement to the Ministry of Digitalisation and Public Governance on the draft Act on Artificial Intelligence, which fully implements the EU AI Act into Norwegian law (noting that Norway is not a member of the EU). Datatilsynet requested clarification of jurisdictional rules for cross-border and domestic AI use and expressed readiness to serve as the market surveillance authority for high-risk AI systems in law enforcement, contingent on receiving additional resources to fulfil these duties. Datatilsynet also proposed a general ban on biometric remote identification, including for commercial use, citing serious privacy and human rights concerns.

[18 June 2025] Nordic Council of Ministers approve funding for a Nordic-Baltic AI Center:
The Nordic Council of Ministers (Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, Sweden) have approved 30 million DKK over three years for starting up and establishing a Nordic Baltic AI-center. Organisations from all Nordic countries (including AI Sweden, AI Finland, Digital Dogme, IKT Norge and Almannarómur) will participate in establishing the Center, which will be formally launched in the fall of 2025. The center will accelerate AI adoption and innovation across the region by acting as a cross-sectoral hub for governments, ecosystems, companies, and organizations. It will consolidate strategic leadership and expertise, oﬀering capabilities needed by national actors. The work will be coordinated by a secretariat located in Sweden. The ambition is to also have the Baltic countries join.

[8 November 2024] Norway launches 2024-2030 national digital strategy, covering AI:
Norway has released its new National Digital Strategy 2024-2030, with a focus on AI. Among other points, the strategy mentions that the Norwegian government will (1) implement the EU AI Act into Norwegian law and establish a national supervisory authority and administrative structure for AI; (2) ensure that 80 per cent of government agencies have adopted AI in 2025, and 100 per cent by 2030; (3) work to further develop a national infrastructure for AI that will provide access to foundational models based on Norwegian and Sámi languages and social conditions; (4) investigate the need for high-performance computing based on known needs in the research sector, public and business sectors; (5) implement changes to sectoral legislation due to the widespread adoption of AI in society; (6) investigate how to use data and text from government agencies for ethical and safe training of national language models; (7) clarify what constitutes the lawful use of copyright and other protected works in text and data mining processes; (8) strengthen the guidance work for the responsible development and use of AI, including through regulatory sandboxes; (9) ensure responsible development and use of AI in the public sector; (10) contribute to better and easier access to health data and the use of AI for the further development of common health service, research and business development; (11) facilitate innovation based on AI in the business sector through the business-oriented policy instrument; (12) ensure that Norway takes an active role internationally to influence the development of legislation in the field of AI especially in terms of the ethical and safe use of AI; and (13) establish research centres for the development and use of AI in society and strengthen quantum technology research.

[28 October 2024] Global data protection authorities issue statement on data scraping, covering AI:
A coalition of 17 global data protection authorities have issued a follow-up statement on data scraping (Australia, Canada, United Kingdom, Hong Kong, China, Switzerland, Norway, New Zealand, Colombia, Jersey, Morocco, Argentina, Mexico, Guernsey, Spain, Monaco, Israel). The statement builds on a joint statement by the authorities in August 2023 urging social media platforms to implement safeguards against data scraping. This follow-up statement specifies that (1) organizations should deploy a combination of safeguarding measures against unlawful scraping, regularly reviewing and updating them to keep pace with advances in scraping techniques and technologies; (2) while AI is used by some sophisticated data scrapers to evade detection, it can also enhance protections against unlawful scraping; (3) the obligation to protect against unlawful scraping applies to both large corporations and SMEs, with lower-cost measures available for SMEs with assistance from service providers; (4) contractual terms authorizing scraping of personal data cannot alone render such scraping lawful, but they can serve as an important safeguard; (5) organizations permitting scraping of personal data must ensure they have a lawful basis, are transparent, and obtain consent where required by law; (6) organizations should implement adequate measures, including contractual terms and monitoring, to ensure compliance with data protection and privacy laws; (7) providing access via an API can allow organizations greater control over data and facilitate detection and mitigation of unauthorized scraping; and (8) organizations using scraped data or their own platform data to train AI must comply with data protection, privacy laws, and AI-specific laws, following available regulatory guidelines.

[4 June 2024] Norway DPA issues note regarding Meta’s use of user content for AI training:
The Norwegian Data Protection Authority (Datatilsynet) issued a note following Meta’s announcement that it will use users' photos and posts from Facebook and Instagram to train its AI services starting 26 June 2024, excluding private messages. The Datatilsynet stated that it has received a complaint about Meta's new data practice and that it expects to receive more in the upcoming weeks. Further, the Datatilsynet noted that the data supervisory authorities from the European Economic Area (EEA) will collaborate to assess Meta's practices.

[22 March 2024] Norway Data Protection Authority publishes strategy on the use of AI:
The Norwegian Data Protection Authority published its Strategy for the Work with AI. The strategy aims to ensure responsible development and use of AI, taking into account individual rights and societal values. It is built on two pillars, external and internal. Externally, the strategy aims to actively contribute to responsible AI development, share experiences through a dialogue-based channel, collaborate with external partners, and stay updated on new legislation related to AI. Internally, the strategy aims to build expertise, ensure coordination across the organisation, establish guidelines for the use of AI tools and develop inspection methodologies for AI.

[20 June 2023] Forbrukerrådet releases generative AI risks report:
The Norwegian Consumer Council (Forbrukerrådet) published its report on the risks and possible harms that generative AI may cause to consumers. The report provides an analysis of generative AI-related harms, legal frameworks, and possible ways forward from a legal, ethical, and political perspective. With regards to privacy harms, the report outlines that challenges arise both in relation to the data sets used for model training and the generated content.

Regulatory Sandbox (2020):
Norwegian Data Protection Authority established a regulatory sandbox for the development of responsible AI. The sandbox provides free guidance to a handful of carefully selected companies, of varying types and sizes, across different sectors, in exchange for full openness about the assessments that are made.

The Norwegian AI (Artificial Intelligence) Strategy (2020):
Norway's national AI strategy which includes 7 AI ethical principles: (1) AI-based solutions must respect human autonomy and control; (2) AI-based systems must be safe and technically robust; (3) AI must take privacy and data protection into account; (4) AI-based systems must be transparent; (5) AI systems must facilitate inclusion, diversity and equal treatment; (6) AI must benefit society and the environment; (7) Accountability.
