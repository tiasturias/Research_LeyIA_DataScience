United Kingdom
Regulation Status
No AI-specific regulation yet. However, AI Opportunities Action Plan indicates that the current government intends to (1) propose legislation that regulates the critical risks associated with next-gen "most powerful" AI models (though this seems to be delayed); (2) establish AI Security Institute (formerly known as AI Safety Institute) as a statutory body; and (3) other various pro-innovation regulatory initiatives to transform UK's sectoral regulators from one of regulating to that of positively promoting innovation. There is also an ongoing consultation to reform the UK text and data mining regime which is relevant to AI training. A private member's bill (Artificial Intelligence (Regulation) Bill) has also been proposed in March 2025. Ongoing developments on the Data (Use and Access) Bill on copyright infringement, AI models, and transparency over inputs.
Regulation by sector approach. The UK relies on existing regulators to interpret, implement and enforce various AI ethics principles (set out in the National Whitepaper on AI Regulation) in their relevant sector.
Hosted Global AI Safety Summit (2023), establishing the Bletchley Declaration and UK AI Safety Institute.

[8 December 2025] UK cyber agency warns LLMs will always be vulnerable to prompt injection:
UK’s top cybersecurity agency - the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSS) - has warned that prompt injection attacks, which exploit vulnerabilities in large language models (LLMs) by manipulating their inputs to produce unintended outputs, are fundamentally different from traditional SQL injection attacks. While SQL injection involves inserting malicious code into a database query to manipulate or access data, prompt injection targets the behaviour of LLMs by crafting inputs that cause the model to disregard its original instructions. This can lead to the generation of harmful content, unauthorized data disclosure, or unintended actions within systems that rely on LLMs. The NCSS suggests a better approach to mitigating prompt injection might be to not treat it as a form of code injection, but instead view it as an exploitation of 'confused deputy vulnerabilities' (i.e. where a system can be coerced to perform a function that benefits the attacker, typically where a privileged component is coerced into making a privileged request on behalf of a less-privileged attacker). Addressing prompt injection requires distinct strategies (with recommendations aligned with the ETSI standard (TS 104 223) on Baseline Cyber Security Requirements for AI Models and Systems), as conventional security measures effective against SQL injection may not be applicable.

Algorithmic Transparency Standard (2023):
A recording standard that helps public sector bodies provide clear information about the algorithmic tools they use and why they are using them. The standard is one of the world's first policies for transparency on the use of algorithmic tools in goverment decision making. It comprises an algorithmic transparency data standard and an algorithmic transparency template and guidance that helps public sector organisations provide information to the data standard.
