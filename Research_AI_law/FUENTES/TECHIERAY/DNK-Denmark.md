[3 February 2026] European Commission issues opinion on Danish Bill amending Copyright Act, introducing performance protection and protection against digitally generated imitations:
The European Commission has issued an opinion under Article 5(2) of Directive (EU) 2015/1535 regarding Denmark’s draft Act amending the Copyright Act, which introduces two new forms of copyright protection: a general right against the public availability of realistic digitally generated imitations of personal characteristics without consent, and a related right allowing performers to control the public availability of imitations of their performances. The Commission expressed concerns about the scope of the new related right, its interaction with existing EU copyright laws, the proposed 50-year protection term post-mortem, and the liability regime for online content-sharing services. Additionally, the Commission emphasized the need for alignment with the EU AI Act, stating that the required labelling should not affect the assessment of resemblance or confusion regarding the protection of digitally generated imitations. The Commission urged Denmark to revise the draft Act to ensure compliance with EU copyright legislation and the EU AI Act.

[26 June 2025] Ministry of Culture proposes to amend Copyright Act prohibiting unauthorised sharing of digitally generated imitations of personal characteristics:
The Danish Ministry of Culture has announced a proposal to amend the Copyright Act prohibiting unauthorised sharing of realistic, digitally generated imitations of personal characteristics. The proposal applies to creators, distributors, and hosting platforms involved in sharing manipulated content that falsely depicts individuals or artists. It introduces two key protections, a general ban on sharing digital imitations of personal traits, including appearance and voice, without consent, and specific protection for performing artists against unauthorised use of their performances. The rules will support the enforcement of takedown obligations under the European Union's Digital Services Act and enable individuals to request content removal, although no criminal penalties will apply.

[18 June 2025] Nordic Council of Ministers approve funding for a Nordic-Baltic AI Center:
The Nordic Council of Ministers (Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, Sweden) have approved 30 million DKK over three years for starting up and establishing a Nordic Baltic AI-center. Organisations from all Nordic countries (including AI Sweden, AI Finland, Digital Dogme, IKT Norge and Almannarómur) will participate in establishing the Center, which will be formally launched in the fall of 2025. The center will accelerate AI adoption and innovation across the region by acting as a cross-sectoral hub for governments, ecosystems, companies, and organizations. It will consolidate strategic leadership and expertise, oﬀering capabilities needed by national actors. The work will be coordinated by a secretariat located in Sweden. The ambition is to also have the Baltic countries join.
