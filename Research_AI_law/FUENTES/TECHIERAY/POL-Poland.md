[23 April 2026] President of Personal Data Protection Office submits opinion on proposal for Act on Artificial Intelligence systems:
The President of the Polish Office for Personal Data Protection has submitted an opinion to the Parliament regarding the draft Act on Artificial Intelligence systems, which was adopted by the Council of Ministers on 31 March 2026, and which pertains to operators and deployers of high-risk AI systems in Poland. The Office welcomed the removal of provisions that infringed on its exclusive data protection competences but criticized the draft's cooperation provisions between the Office and the new Commission for the Development and Security of Artificial Intelligence as lacking legal substance, failing to define the form, scope, or procedures for inter-authority collaboration. The opinion further advocates for the Office's role as a fundamental rights protection body under the European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act to be explicitly established in law and for the Office to be granted a formal right to present reasoned positions on data processing issues prior to the Commission issuing binding decisions.

[31 October 2024] Poland designates three public bodies to enforce protections of fundamental rights related to high-risk AI systems under the EU AI Act:
The Ministry of Digital Affairs has designated three public bodies to oversee and enforce protections of fundamental rights related to high-risk AI systems under the EU AI Act, namely the Children's Rights Ombudsman, the Patients' Rights Ombudsman, and the National Labor Inspectorate. For clarity, the authorities will not serve as competent authorities under the Act, but will gain additional powers to support their existing mandates in cases involving AI systems (e.g. permitted to access documentation on the use of high-risk AI systems to ensure compliance with these protections)

[16 October 2024] Poland unveils draft law implementing EU AI Act:
The Ministry of Digital Affairs has opened a public consultation on the draft law to implement the EU AI Act. The draft law will cover (1) establishment of the AI Development and Security Commission – a new, collegial body that will monitor the market and support entrepreneurs; (2) designation of the Social Council as a consultative body – composed of representatives of non-governmental organisations, trade unions and entrepreneurs, to co-shape AI policy in Poland and ensure broad participation in this process; (3) popularization of the use of AI systems, including high-risk systems – ensuring broad access to AI technologies while taking into account user safety. Including the creation of regulatory sandboxes; (4) general and individual interpretations for companies – to ensure stability and predictability of the operations of enterprises that use AI technologies; (5) annual AI computing and energy intelligence – to ensure transparency, sustainability and help plan AI investments; and (6) recommendations for best practices in the use of AI – promoting ethical and effective methods of implementing AI technologies in various sectors.  Consultation closes on 15 November 2024.

[4 March 2026] Deputy President of the Personal Data Protection Office speaks on the role of AI in local governments:
The Deputy President of the Personal Data Protection Office, Konrad Komornicki, participated in a panel at the 11th European Congress of Local Governments in Mikołajki, discussing the role of AI in public administration. The debate, titled "Artificial Intelligence as a Tool of the Future for Public Offices," highlighted the practical implementation of AI in local governments to enhance administrative efficiency, data analysis, and public service quality, while clarifying that AI is intended to support rather than replace officials. Komornicki stressed the importance of aligning technological advancements with personal data protection and public trust, emphasizing the need for legal compliance, data reliability, and accuracy in AI applications. The European Congress serves as a significant forum for addressing local governance challenges, including digital transformation and sustainable development, organized by the Institute for Eastern Studies Foundation.

[18 September 2025] Personal Data Protection Office's proposal to introduce law to address dissemination of harmful deepfake content:
The President of the Personal Data Protection Office (UODO) has presented a proposal to introduce a law protecting individuals from harmful deepfakes. The proposal focuses on technology companies and social media platforms hosting manipulated images, audio, or video of identifiable persons. It proposes legal accountability for creating and sharing deepfakes, stronger personal data protection, mandatory platform detection and labelling systems, rapid removal of illegal content, and public education on deepfake risks. It was highlighted that current Polish law provides partial protection against deepfakes through copyright, civil, penal, and Artificial Intelligence regulations, but does not fully address their specific risks. Key gaps include limited liability rules for creators, insufficient platform obligations, and lack of fast mechanisms to remove harmful content.

[6 February 2025] UODO adopts guidance cautioning against use of DeepSeek:
The Office of Data Protection Commissioner (UODO) has adopted an outline cautioning against the use of DeepSeek over data protection concerns. The outline (1) warned that user data may be stored on servers in China, which lacks an adequacy decision from the European Union (EU); (2) highlighted that Chinese authorities have broad data access without EU-equivalent protections; and (3) noted that generative AI models process vast amounts of data, potentially using it for unintended purposes such as training or marketing.

[31 December 2025] Poland urges EU to probe TikTok over AI-generated content suspected to come from Russia:
It is reported that Poland Deputy Digitalisation Minister Dariusz has requested the European Commission to investigate TikTok for hosting AI-generated content, including videos urging Poland to withdraw from the EU, which officials believe is likely Russian disinformation. A TikTok profile featuring young women in Polish national colors promoting EU withdrawal gained popularity but has since been removed. The Deputy Digitalisation Minister emphasized that such content threatens public order and democratic processes, suggesting TikTok may be failing to meet its obligations as a Very Large Online Platform. A government spokesperson noted that the recordings contained Russian syntax, indicating their origin. TikTok stated it has been in contact with Polish authorities and has removed content violating its rules.

[17 September 2025] Data protection authorities adopted joint statement on building trustworthy data governance frameworks to encourage development of innovative and privacy-protecting AI:
Twenty (20) data protection authorities, including from Australia, Belgium, Canada, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Ireland, Italy, Korea, Netherlands, New Zealand, Luxembourg, Spain, and UK adopted the joint statement on building trustworthy data governance frameworks to encourage development of innovative and privacy-protecting AI. The statement recognises the opportunities and risks of AI, including discrimination, misinformation, and hallucination from inappropriate data use, and stresses embedding privacy by design, strong governance, and transparency. The statement commits to clarifying lawful grounds for AI training data and exchanging information on proportionate safety measures. It also focuses on monitoring technical and societal impacts with contributions from non-governmental organisations, public authorities, academia, and businesses, and reducing legal uncertainties through regulatory sandboxes and best practice sharing.
