[30 June 2025] Greek Ministry of Digital Governance renamed to include AI:
According to the Prime Minister's meeting schedule and other news sources, it is known that Greek Prime Minister Kyriakos Mitsotakis has announced that the Ministry of Digital Governance will now be called the Ministry of Digital Governance and Artificial Intelligence. Minister Dimitris Papastergiou has also (1) unveiled a strategic plan for AI, which includes establishing a Secretariat for Artificial Intelligence and Data Governance; and (2) initiated a €25 million program to integrate AI solutions into the public sector.

[29 May 2025] HDPA closes investigation into DeepSeek:
The Hellenic Data Protection Authority (HDPA) has closed its investigation into the DeepSeek Artificial Intelligence application after Hangzhou DeepSeek Artificial Intelligence Co., Ltd. informed the Authority on 28 May 2025 that it had appointed Prighter EU Rep GmbH, based in Vienna, as its representative in the European Union, in accordance with Article 27 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The appointment followed Interim Ruling 18/2025, in which the HDPA found the company to be within the territorial scope of the GDPR and ordered it to designate an EU-based representative.

[28 November 2024] Greece releases national AI blueprint:
The High Level Advisory Committee on AI has published the national blueprint for AI transformation for Greece. The blueprint (1) examines how AI can promote innovation and enhance the business ecosystem, positioning Greece as an attractive hub for startups and investments; (2) emphasizes strengthening education and research, essential for developing skills and advancing knowledge in the AI sector; (3) explores the regulatory framework, emphasizing the importance of ensuring responsible and safe use of AI; and (4) proposes ways to apply AI technologies in the public sector to improve efficiency and transparency. On the regulatory aspect, the blueprint recommends (a) establishment of a National AI Supervisory Authority to protect human rights and strengthen AI innovation and research; (b) transforming the Data Protection Authority into a Data Protection Authority and Access to Information Authority to ensure a balance between personal data protection and access to information; and (c) updating existing laws to harmonise with the EU AI Act.

[12 November 2024] Authorities appointed to oversee EU AI Act in Greece:
The Greek Ministry of Digital Governance has announced the Hellenic Data Protection Authority, The Ombudsman, the Hellenic Authority for Communication Security and Privacy, and the National Commission for Human Rights as designated authorities tasked with overseeing compliance with the EU AI Act. These designated authorities will receive additional powers by 2 August 2026 to access necessary documentation for ensuring compliance with the EU AI Act.
