[4 August 2025] 2025 APEC Digital and AI Ministerial Statement:
At the APEC summit in Incheon, South Korea, the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation adopted the 2025 Digital and AI Ministerial Statement, highlighting the role of AI in driving innovation and addressing socio-economic challenges. The statement emphasises secure and responsible adoption of AI and other digital technologies to enhance productivity, efficiency, resilience, and economic development. It encourages member economies to integrate these technologies with balanced, human-centred approaches to workforce development, education, and lifelong learning. [25 November 2024] New updated draft of Law No. 31814 released:
The Peru Secretariat of Government and Digital Transformation has opened a consultation on a new draft of the "Regulation of Law No. 31814 promoting the use of AI for the economic and social development of the country". The new draft Regulation reaffirms prior requirements, including (1) classification of AI systems based on risk levels and prohibitions on the use of AI systems that are classified as unacceptable risk; (2) incorporation of security measures throughout the lifecycle of AI systems, including risk management approaches, backup systems, algorithmic audits, and the reporting of security incidents in AI-based systems; and (3) measures requiring the protection of privacy in AI systems and transparency in the operation and decision-making parameters. The new draft Regulation also includes new provisions for visible labelling on AI-generated text, images, videos and audio. The consultation closes on 6 December 2024.

[10 August 2024] Latin American countries adopt sweeping AI declaration:
Representatives of 17 Latin American countries (Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Cuba, Curaçao, Ecuador, Guatemala, Guyana, Honduras, Panama, Paraguay, Peru, Dominican Republic, Suriname and Uruguay) have signed the Cartagena Declaration on AI governance. The declaration includes (1) adherence to UNESCO's ethics recommendations from the AI for Good Global Summit, (2) alignment with existing soft law instruments like the OECD's AI recommendations, and (3) continuation of similar AI initiatives previously signed in Montevideo and Santiago. The text of the declaration has yet to be publicly revealed.

[15 February 2024] Peru advances with EU-inspired AI regulation:
Peruvian lawmakers are discussing a proposal to establish a framework for the use and development of AI technologies, modeled after the European Union’s AI rules. Submitted by lawmaker Carlos Javier Zeballos Madariaga, this bill will (1) apply to AI technologies in Peru and those hosted on data centers overseas but offered and applied in Peru, (2) categorize AI risks into four types based on their impact on essential or human rights (unacceptable, high, limited, and insignificant), and (3) align with Peru's personal data regulation, requiring clear and explicit consent for the collection and processing of users' data.
