[20 February 2026] Estonia launches AI technical & regulatory sandbox:
The Estonia Ministry of Justice and Digital Affairs has established an artificial intelligence test environment to facilitate the development, testing, and deployment of AI solutions in a legitimate, transparent, and trustworthy manner, aligning with the EU AI Act. This controlled environment allows organizations to assess high-risk AI systems, ensuring compliance with requirements such as risk assessment, transparency, and documentation, thereby reducing legal and technical risks while enhancing social trust and innovation. The test environment offers regulatory support, including legal advice and risk management, alongside technical assistance for testing and validating AI solutions. Participation begins with a preliminary assessment to determine risk levels, followed by a tailored testing plan, with real-condition testing expected to commence in the second half of 2026. The service is free for public authorities and small to medium-sized enterprises, aiming to promote reliable, human-centered AI development in Estonia.

[6 February 2025] Ministers rule out giving Estonian content to AI companies for free:
It is reported that Estonian Prime Minister Kristen Michal and Minister of Culture Heidy Purga have both ruled out the possibility of handing over Estonian-language data, including media content belonging to ERR News (i.e. English-language service of Estonian Public Broadcasting), to AI companies for free. Minister of Education and Research Kristina Kallas said this is a really important issue for the future of the Estonian language, as the corpus currently available in Estonian for use in large language models is very limited. The idea had been backed by Minister of Justice and Digital Affairs Lisa Pakosta.
